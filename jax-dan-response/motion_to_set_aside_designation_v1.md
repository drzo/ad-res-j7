## I. URGENCY

This matter is urgent. The fraudulent designation of the First Respondent as "Main Trustee" was a calculated step to seize control of the Trust ahead of the **ZAR 18.75 million Ketoni payout** in May 2026. With only months remaining, any decisions made by the First Respondent under this false authority could irrevocably prejudice the Applicants' rights and imperil the Trust's largest asset. The Court's urgent intervention is required to prevent this.

## II. INTRODUCTION

1.  This is an application to set aside a fraudulent, backdated trustee designation and to remove the trustees responsible for this misconduct. The application is brought by a trustee and a capital beneficiary of the Faucitt Family Trust.

2.  The application is founded on the following grounds:
    a.  **Fraud and Forgery / Post-Signature Alteration:** The designation document was represented as having been effective from **1 July 2025**, but the Applicants contend that no such trustees' decision occurred on that date and that the "1 July 2025" date is suspected to have been **added, printed, or superimposed after signature**, creating a false record and granting the First Respondent authority he did not possess at the time.
    b.  **Breach of Fiduciary Duty:** The First Respondent acted dishonestly and against the interests of the beneficiaries by relying on a document with a disputed effective date to assert overriding authority.
    c.  **Abuse of Process:** The disputed document was used to launch vexatious litigation against the Applicants.
    d.  **Lack of Authority:** The Trust Deed does not authorize the creation of a "Main Trustee" position.
    e.  **Irregular Appointment of Bantjies (Alleged):** The Third Respondent, **DANIEL JACOBUS BANTJIES**, is alleged to have been appointed as a trustee through an irregular process and/or without proper authority, allegedly at the instance of **Rynette**, which appointment is disputed and has contributed to an unworkable trusteeship and prejudice to beneficiaries.

## III. DISPUTED EFFECTIVE DATE AND SUSPECTED POST-SIGNATURE ALTERATION

3.  On **August 11, 2025**, the Second Respondent signed a document relating to the purported designation of the First Respondent as "Main Trustee."

4.  The document was later presented as having been effective from **July 1, 2025**.

5.  No meeting of trustees occurred on July 1, 2025, and no such decision was made on that date. The document was used to portray a set of facts that did not occur.

6.  The Applicants further contend that the "July 1, 2025" effective date is suspected to have been **applied after signature**, including by being printed onto or over a page that had already been signed, or otherwise applied in a manner inconsistent with contemporaneous execution.

7.  The timing remains highly material. The document was relied upon shortly thereafter when the First Respondent launched an interdict application against the Applicants on **August 13, 2025**, relying on the authority purportedly granted by the disputed designation.

## IV. GROUNDS FOR RELIEF

### A. Setting Aside the Designation (Relief 1, 2, 3)

8.  **Fraud Vitiates All / Unreliable Instrument:** A document born of fraud, forgery, or material post-signature alteration is a nullity. The designation is void *ab initio*. All actions flowing from it, including the interdict, are likewise void and must be set aside.

9.  **Breach of Fiduciary Duty:** The act of creating, using, or relying on a designation with a disputed effective date to seize control of the Trust is a profound breach of the duty of honesty and good faith owed by a trustee.

10. **Lack of Authority:** The Applicants will argue, in the alternative, that the Trust Deed does not permit the creation of a "Main Trustee" with overriding powers, rendering the designation invalid regardless of the date.

### B. Removal of Trustees (Relief 4, 5)

11. **Section 20(1) of the Trust Property Control Act** permits the Court to remove a trustee whose conduct imperils the trust property or is detrimental to the beneficiaries. (*Gowar v Gowar*).

12. **Grounds for Removal of Peter Faucitt (First Respondent):**
    *   He orchestrated and/or relied upon the disputed designation to seize personal control of the Trust.
    *   He abused his position to launch vexatious litigation against his co-beneficiaries.
    *   His conduct demonstrates he is unfit to hold the office of trustee.

13. **Grounds for Removal of Daniel Jacobus Bantjies (Third Respondent) (Alleged):**
    *   The Third Respondent's appointment is alleged to have been procured through an irregular process and/or without proper authority, allegedly at the instance of **Rynette**, and is disputed.
    *   His continued role as trustee in these circumstances imperils the proper administration of the Trust and is detrimental to beneficiaries, including by entrenching an unworkable and conflicted trusteeship.
    *   The Applicants reserve the right to supplement these grounds upon receipt of the underlying appointment documents and any correspondence with the Master.

## V. CONCLUSION

The First Respondent has engaged in a calculated course of conduct to seize control of the Faucitt Family Trust for personal gain, using a designation document with a disputed effective date and suspected post-signature alteration. The Court's urgent intervention is required to remedy this injustice, restore the proper administration of the Trust, and protect its assets before they are irrevocably compromised.
